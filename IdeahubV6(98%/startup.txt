IdeaHub — System Overview for Your Client
What IdeaHub is
IdeaHub is a web-based management system for a student-focused café / workspace business. Staff use it in the browser (computer or tablet) to:

Check customers in and out of lounge spaces
Take food and drink orders
Record Cash and GCash payments
Track inventory, expenses, and money owed
Produce daily sales and balance reports for the owner and accountant
All important data is stored in a central database (MySQL), so records stay in one place instead of scattered notebooks or spreadsheets.

A typical day — step by step
1. Customer arrives (check-in)
On the Dashboard, staff tap + (New Customer Check-in) and enter:

Customer name
School and course (optional, for records)
Number of people
Space type: Regular Lounge, Premium Lounge, or Boardroom
The system:

Starts a session (visit timer)
Checks seat capacity so you do not overbook a lounge
Shows the customer on the dashboard with a running bill (time + any orders)
2. During the visit — food and drinks
Staff open Orders for that customer’s session and add items from the menu.

Only available items can be ordered; unavailable items show clearly
When admin disables an item on the menu, it updates for staff ordering in real time
Inventory can automatically go down when orders are placed; low stock alerts can appear
3. Checkout (customer leaves)
Staff open the customer’s bill and choose Checkout.

They must select payment method:

Cash
GCash
A confirmation dialog appears (no old browser pop-ups). After confirm:

The visit is closed
A transaction is saved (total bill, space charge, food charge, payment method)
A receipt can be viewed/printed
The customer disappears from the active dashboard list
4. End of shift / end of day (admin)
Admin goes to Daily Sales Balance and:

Generates a daily report for a chosen date (revenue, expenses, net balance, order and session counts)
Can also record soft balancing (AM / PM shift snapshots)
Exports PDF — best for the client or accountant (easy to open on phone or email)
Exports Excel or CSV — for deeper analysis in Excel
The PDF includes:

Total revenue, expenses, net balance
Cash vs GCash totals and how many checkouts used each
A per-day table with Cash and GCash columns
Soft balancing history (if used)

Important for the client:

GCash and Cash are recorded at checkout, not guessed later.
Older checkouts from before GCash was added may show as Cash by default.
Real-time updates (why screens stay in sync)
When one computer changes the menu, inventory, expenses, or receivables, other logged-in devices can refresh automatically (live updates over the network).

The dashboard refreshes active sessions on a short interval so bills stay current while customers are still inside.

Security and access (simple terms)
Password login required
Sessions expire after inactivity (about 24 hours configured)
Admin-only pages blocked for normal staff
CSRF protection on forms (helps prevent fake submissions from other sites)
Rate limiting on login (helps against password guessing)
Production should use a strong secret key and HTTPS on the live server.

Where data lives
Primary database: MySQL (e.g. Railway in production)
Backups: Client should arrange regular database backups with their host (Railway / provider)
Exports: PDF/Excel are copies for audit; the database remains the source of truth
How it is built (one paragraph for technical stakeholders)
IdeaHub is a Python Flask application with a layered design: screens → business logic → database access → stored tables. It is roughly 70% structured in that pattern (services and repositories), which makes it maintainable as the business grows. The user interface is HTML pages with modern styling (Bootstrap); no separate phone app is required—only a web browser.

What IdeaHub is not
Not a full accounting system (no full chart of accounts, payroll, or BIR tax filing built in)
Not automatic bank/GCash API sync—you record GCash when staff selects it at checkout
Not Google Sheets sync yet (planned as a future phase; exports cover audit needs today)
Suggested workflow for the client (owner + accountant)
Daily (staff): Check-in → orders → checkout with correct Cash or GCash.

Daily (admin): Review Checkout Records → generate Daily Sales Balance for that date.

Weekly/monthly: Export PDF from Daily Balance → send to accountant. Use Analytics for trends.

As needed: Update menu prices, log expenses, track receivables, adjust inventory.

IdeaHub — Quick Start (Staff & Owner)
Login: your shop link → Login

For the owner (30 seconds)
IdeaHub tracks who’s inside, orders, and Cash vs GCash at checkout. Staff follow the 4 steps below. You review Checkout Records anytime and Daily Balance → Export PDF at end of day for your accountant.

Every shift — 4 steps
Log in → you see the Dashboard (active customers).

Check in — tap +, enter name, number of people, pick Regular / Premium / Boardroom, tap Check In.
If it says full, that space has no seats left.

Orders (if they buy food/drink) — Orders menu → add items. Don’t use gray / “Not Available” items.

Checkout — open their bill → choose Cash or GCash → Confirm.
Always pick how they actually paid — reports depend on this.
